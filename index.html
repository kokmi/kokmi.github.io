Conclusions

To be honest, Hispanic males in the southern Washington Heights perform more poorly than females. However, this "poorer performance depends how to evaluate their achivements. Specifically, when considering in the whole context, males earn more than females in common. Nevertheless, the individual income of men in the Heights stays in a lower level according to the nation average. Therefore, Hispanic males seem to behave worse in the Heights eventhough they earn more. This "poor behavior" can also be reflected in the employment rate of different genders, while Hispanic women who grew up here will have higher employment rate in their adults. Despite similar possibilities of still staying in the neighborhood, Hispanic men are willing to stay in the same tract rather than go out to find work opportunities. Therefore, the work opportunities are much more limited and competitive for Hispanic males who grew up here.

Concentrating on opportunities for people from different family origins , individuals from high-income families earn more in their adults. Probably, they get more access to excellent education quality and are more skillful in workforce.
